Hello everyone, welcome to my website!

This is Donya, I am a senior student of both EE-Communication (Major) and CE (Minor) in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Tehran.

I am no genius; there is no special talent in me, and no, I do not know it all (I wish I did). I am just passionately curious about the world around me and very enthusiastic about math, so I want to try [persistently and hard] to understand it all a bit better every day as long as I am alive; that is all; that is my goal. One once said, "Beautiful math tends to be useful, and useful things tend to have beautiful math," during my studies and experiences, I came to understand that saying a bit! Observing how math, as abstract as it is, can solve problems, real-world problems, and how it can actually make the world around us comprehensible is just fascinating to me! My courses, along with my research experiences, made me realize that I ultimately wanted to use abstract knowledge to solve real-world problems, and maybe more specifically, Machine Learning problems. Along the way, I would like to use my knowledge of signal processing, algebra, statistics, information theory, various algorithm design methods, and data structures as my primary tools.

"The beauty of mathematics only shows itself to more patient followers," and I would be lying if I said I was patient enough during my undergrad. I wanted to explore and experience as much as possible, touch different problems, get familiar with various fields, and of course, build a strong background for graduate school. I took various theoretical courses and worked as an intern in different places, from the Sharif University of Technology, Iran, to the Max Planck Institute, Germany, and the University of Texas at Austin, USA. I had the opportunity to get to know fantastic people from all around the world, work with them, and learn from them, resulting in a paper accepted in ICML 2021 Self Supervised Learning Workshop, with an extension that will soon be submitted [to VLDB 2022].

I was fortunate to work on my Bachelor's Thesis under the supervision of Prof. Keshav Pingali from the University of Texas at Austin. I am the first Bachelor student in my university who has officially done her thesis internationally.
